Inspirational Women in Tech

Welcome to the Inspirational Women in Tech page! Role models are important for any field, and in the field of technology women have, for centuries, been an influential presence. The work of numerous women through the years has helped to shape the direction and vision of technological advances. This page aims to highlight the achievements of some of these important women, and to encourage others to pursue a career in STEM.

Ada Lovelace

It would be remiss of any page dedicated to inspirational women in tech not to start with Ada Lovelace. As the child of Lord Byron, she was given extensive tuition in mathematics and science. Through her later friendship with Charles Baggage, she translated a French article on his "Analytical Engine", which resembled a modern computer. Her extensive notes on that project were later used by Alan Turing as inspiration for his work in the 1940s.
You can read more about her life and work here.

Reshma Saujani

Reshma Saujani is the founder and CEO of Girls Who Code, a non-profit organisation that works to try and close the gender gap in the tech industry. She spoke at the 2017 American Library Association Conference in support of programmes that specifically targeted girls entering computer science.
More information about Girls Who Code can be found here.

Ellen K. Pao

Ellen K. Pao is the co-founder and CEO of Project Include, which is a non-profit organisation that is focused on improving diversity within the tech industry. She is a former CEO of Reddit and has written for the New York Times and The Washington Post.
More information on Project Include can be found here.

Kimberly Bryant

Kimberly Bryant is the founder and CEO of Black Girls Code, which strives to increase the number of women in colour in tech. In 2013, she was recognised as a White House Champion of Change for Tech Inclusion, and in 2014, she received the American Ingenuity Award for Social Progress from the Smithsonian Magazine.
More information regarding the Black Girls Code website can be found here.
